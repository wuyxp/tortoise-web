## Y1 "The Voyage Begins"

**Core Theme**: You are not starting this journey alone.

### Prologue — The First Departure
*The day we set out, Captain Tort walked into the sea.*
*He rarely went into the water — he said the island needed him, that his own far travels had ended long ago. But that time, he walked until the water was waist-deep, stopped, and looked back at Lil' Grey.*
*"Come," he said, "I want to show you something."*
*The sand at Sandbell Beach was soft, making a tiny crunching sound with every step. Captain Tort looked down, brushed away a layer of shallow sand, and revealed a small shell — it was sleeping, its color almost identical to the sand.*
*"You try," he said to Lil' Grey.*
*Lil' Grey wasn't sure what he could do. But he approached the shell and placed his hand on it.*
*Whether it was the light he brought or something else — that little shell slowly began to glow.*
*Captain Tort watched for a long time without speaking.*
*Finally, he said: "Good. You will do it. I always knew you would."*
*That was Lil' Grey's first departure. And it was Captain Tort's last time in the water. From then on, he stayed by the island's edge — waiting for Lil' Grey to return and tell him the stories.*

## Y2 "Distant Tides"

**Core Theme**: Getting lost isn't a mistake; it's entering a place that hasn't been named yet.

### Prologue · The Entrance to the Mist
*Lil' Grey saw that mist — it wasn't the first time. It was always at the edge of the map — most of the Time Mist had receded, but that area always had a ring of white that wouldn't clear.*
*Captain Tort walked to the island's edge that day, standing closer to the water than usual.*
*"There," he said, looking at the mist, "I went there, but I didn't finish."*
*Lil' Grey waited for him to say more, but he didn't.*
*"Go and see," he said, "see how far you can go."*
*Lil' Grey set out. He didn't ask why Captain Tort hadn't finished. Some questions wait for the answer to walk to you.*

## Y3 "The Echo"

**Core Theme**: You know someone is there, so you dare to move forward.

### Epilogue · You Already Know
*Before the last departure of that year, Lil' Grey walked to Captain Tort, wanting to ask where he should go today.*
*Before he could speak, Captain Tort looked at him.*
*"Where do you want to go today?" Captain Tort asked.*
*Lil' Grey named a place.*
*Captain Tort nodded and said, "Go."*
*Lil' Grey set out. When he returned, Captain Tort was sitting a little distance away. Lil' Grey went over and told him about the day.*
*"You don't need to ask me anymore," Captain Tort said, "you already know."*
***"The island remembers every little tortoise that has walked here. it remembers every time you come, every time you leave. The roads you walk are left here."***

## Y4 "The Sunken Bell"

**Core Theme**: Even in the deepest places, someone has left a light.

### Epilogue · You Went Deeper Than I Did
*Lil' Grey surfaced, swam back to the island, still carrying the chill of that depth.*
*Captain Tort was on the shore, standing straighter than usual.*
*"You reached the bottom," he said, not a question.*
*Lil' Grey said yes, he had touched the bell, and the bell had rung.*
*Captain Tort was silent for a long time.*
*"I only ever went as deep as the lamp," he finally said, "I didn't go any further."*
*He looked at Lil' Grey.*
***"You went deeper than I did."***
*Lil' Grey didn't speak, but he brought back the Whale's lamp and gave it to Captain Tort. Captain Tort took it in his hands, looked down, and watched it for a long time.*

## Y5 "The Ancient Kin"

**Core Theme**: You are part of a very long story.

### Epilogue · Old Things, New Hands
*That year, Lil' Grey kept the star map given by the ancestors in a safe place, taking it out to look at it every once in a while.*
*He didn't know what was in that blank space, but he knew he would find it.*
*Captain Tort walked over one day, saw him looking at the star map, and said:*
***"Old things, left there, are just old things. In new hands, they gain weight again."***
*Lil' Grey folded the map and put it away.*
*He knew Captain Tort wasn't just talking about the map.*

## Y6 "Guardian of the Island"

**Core Theme**: You already know how to look after someone else.

### Epilogue · He Didn't Know When He Had Learned It
*Lil' Tan set out, returned, brought back his first item, and happily told Lil' Grey about it.*
*Lil' Grey listened without interrupting, and when he was done, he nodded and said, "Tell me, what did it feel like there?"*
*He stopped as soon as he said it.*
*He had heard those words elsewhere — Captain Tort had said them to him.*
***Lil' Grey looked at Lil' Tan, using a look he had seen elsewhere. He didn't know when he had learned it.***

## Y7 "The Dawn"

**Core Theme**: I have given you everything I know; the rest, you know better than I do.

### Epilogue · Dawn
*Since then, Lil' Grey has come to the lighthouse every morning to light the lamp.*
*Lil' Tan slowly learned to help him do this too.*
*One morning, Lil' Tan arrived first, and the lamp was already lit. Lil' Grey stood below, looking up, seeing Lil' Tan standing at the top of the lighthouse, like a tiny star pattern, illuminated by the light.*
*Lil' Grey thought, this must have been how Captain Tort looked at him back then.*
***That sea no longer had the Time Mist.***
***Thirty star patterns, and many more after that, were all shining.***
***The lighthouse lamp is lit by Lil' Grey every morning.***
***Lil' Tan stands on top, looking at the ocean.***
*In the distance, perhaps there is a tiny figure, swimming leisurely in that sea, clean for the first time.*
